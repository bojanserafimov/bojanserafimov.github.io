Egg Fried Rice (content warning: sacrilege)

I followed another white boy's recipe and turned my boiled rice leftovers into a full delicious meal in 10 minutes. there's a lot to improve here, but it's good enough to make again.


Quick Prep

We won't have time to look for things while cooking fried rice. It's important to prep everything in advance.

> Get the right pan. You want something that handles heat (some non-stick pans don't) and is roomy enough to allow you to stir the rice. There are many variations on fried rice, but most traditional ones will use a wok. Not sure if you'll get all the benefit of a work if you don't also have a wok stove, wok burner, and mad wok skills. Use what you know. I used a cast iron skillet. It was deep enough to allow me to move the rice around without spilling, but it took a while to heat up.

> Get a tool for stirring. You'll need to scoop rice, stir it, and break up any chunks. I used a spatula, and it was fine but rice clumps were hard to break. I've seen people use the back of a ladle.

> Choose a cooking oil. IMPORTANT: Don't use olive oil! It will burn and give you cancer. Use vegetable oil or canola oil. Something with a high smoke point.

> Get cold boiled rice. It's important for the rice to be cold. I don't know why yet.
> Add cooking oil and sesame oil to the rice
> Separate the grains, otherwise rice will stick later

> Whisk eggs in a bowl
> Chop green onion
> Get soy sauce

Cooking

The goal is to cook the wet stuff first so we don't have excess moisture in the rice, then cook the rice on high heat until it gets toasted, and finally add low-moisture ingredients.

Cook eggs
> Heat a good amount of oil
> Add eggs
> Cook until 70% done

Cook rice
> Add the rice
> Mix and break up any chunks
> Turn the heat to high
> Add a drop of soy sauce and mix it (it should sizzle)
> Cook until rice gets toasted (it should brown a little bit and smell really good)
> Turn off the heat

Season
> Add green onion
> Add salt, pepper
