Egg Fried Rice (content warning: sacrilege)

I followed another white boy's recipe and turned my boiled rice leftovers into a full delicious meal in 10 minutes. there's a lot to improve here, but it's good enough to make again.


You want something that handles heat (some non-stick pans don't) and is roomy enough to allow you to stir the rice. There are many variations on fried rice, but most traditional ones will use a wok. Not sure if you'll get all the benefit of a work if you don't also have a wok stove, wok burner, and mad wok skills. Use what you know. I used a cast iron skillet. It was deep enough to allow me to move the rice around without spilling, but it took a while to heat up.
> Get the right pan

You'll need to scoop rice, stir it, and break up any chunks. I used a spatula, and it was fine but rice clumps were hard to break. I've seen people use the back of a ladle.
> Get a tool for stirring

Prep all the ingredients. You won't have time to do this while cooking.
> Get soy sauce
> Chop green onion
> Whisk eggs in a bowl

It's important for the rice to be cold. I don't know why yet.
> Get boiled rice leftovers from fridge

IMPORTANT: Don't use olive oil, it will burn and give you cancer.
> Add cooking oil and sesame oil to the rice
> Separate the grains (otherwise rice will stick later)

We cook the eggs before the rice so we can get the moisture out of them.
> Heat a good amount of high-temperature oil
> Cook scrambled eggs until 70% done

Now the goal is to mix everything as fast as possible, keep rice grains separated, and then toast them at very high heat which will release a new smell and flavor
> Add the rice
> Break up any chunks
> Turn the heat to high
> Add a drop of soy sauce and mix it (it should sizzle)
> Cook until rice gets toasted. Stir
> turn off the heat

We leave these for the end because we don't want to burn them, and they don't have much moisture so we don't have to cook them first.
> Add green onion
> Season and stir
